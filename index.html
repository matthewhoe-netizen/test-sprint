THE OBJECTIVE

Why Are We Doing This?

Every team has work that is repetitive, slow, and ripe for automation. We've been talking about AI — now we act on it. Two focused days to identify, build, and demonstrate real AI workflows that save your team real time.

"The worst reason to do something is because it's how it was done before. Ask instead: what creates the most value, for the least effort?"
— SHAOWEN · SHOPEE REGIONAL SPX/WH/LOGS
⚡
Stop the Grind
Every team spends hours each week on tasks that are repetitive, low-judgement, and could be handled by AI. We're going to find them and fix them — in two days, not two quarters.
🔍
Identify & Prioritise
Day 1 is about getting honest about where time actually goes. Teams map their pain points, agree on priorities, and commit to what they'll automate.
🛠
Build Something Real
Not a deck. Not a POC that sits in a folder. A working AI workflow your team can use the following Monday. Day 2 is pure build time.
📣
Show Your Work
End of Day 2, every team presents to Shaowen. What did you build? How does it work? How many hours will it save? No hiding — real demos only.

END OF DAY 2 · THE SHOWCASE

No Slides. Just Show It.

The demo is not a formal presentation. No decks. No formatting for the sake of formatting. Walk into Shaowen's room and show her what you built.

🚶
Walk In, Show It
Walk into Shaowen's room between 16:00–18:30 on Day 2. Open your laptop. Show the Claude workflow you built. Run it live. No slides, no build-up — just the thing itself working.
🎯
Three Things to Cover
① What is the process — what were you doing before, how long did it take?
② What you built — show it running live with real or realistic data
③ What's next — how will the team adopt it, who owns it, what's the rollout?
⏱
Keep It Under 10 Minutes
With so many teams, time is tight. Be crisp. The demo should speak for itself — if you need 20 minutes to explain it, it's too complicated. Aim for something simple enough to demo in 5.
💬
Shaowen Will Ask
→ What is new about this?
→ How many hours per week does this save?
→ Is the team already using it?
→ What would it take to roll this out more broadly?

WHAT'S NEXT

This Is Just the Beginning

The sprint is two days. The transformation is ongoing. Here's what happens after Day 2.

📊
We're Tracking AI Usage
After the sprint, we will be monitoring how Claude and other AI tools are being adopted across teams. This includes frequency of use, types of tasks automated, and time saved. The data will help us understand where AI is genuinely embedding into how we work — and where we need more support.
📋
One Component of Your Assessment
Going forward, how you apply AI tools will be one of the ways you can demonstrate your effectiveness and impact. It's not about using AI for its own sake — it's about showing that you're finding smarter ways to work, create value, and grow. The sprint is your starting point.
💡
The Expectation Going Forward
Every team is expected to walk away from this sprint with at least one live Claude workflow running within two weeks. Not a prototype — something your team is actually using. Build it here, own it after.
